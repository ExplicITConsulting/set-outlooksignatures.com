Centralized email signature management should save money, not become another expensive subscription.

The real question is not whether professional signature management costs money.

The real question is:

> Is it cheaper than the manual effort, risk, and missed marketing value it replaces?

## The business case

Every realistic business case for email signature management comes down to three questions:

1. What do incorrect or non-compliant signatures and out-of-office replies cost in reputation, brand trust, or regulatory risk?
2. What is the value of using every outgoing email as a consistent, compliant marketing channel?
3. Is the software cheaper than the manual effort it replaces?

The first two questions depend heavily on the organization and are difficult to quantify precisely.

The third one is easy.

Assume an average employee cost of **EUR 72,000 per year**, including taxes, social security, and overhead. At about **1,800 working hours**, this equals roughly **EUR 0.67 per minute**.

Now calculate the effort required for manual signature updates:

```text
Cost per minute × changes per year × minutes per change × number of signatures × number of clients
```

Example:

```text
0.67 × 2 × 3 × 2 × 1 = EUR 8.04
```

That means manual signature maintenance already costs **EUR 8.04 per user and year** in a very modest scenario.

Set-OutlookSignatures with the Benefactor Circle add-on costs **EUR 3.12 per mailbox and year**.

So even before considering compliance, brand consistency, shared mailboxes, out-of-office replies, outdated contact data, or marketing value, the add-on already pays for itself.

## And the calculation is still conservative

The EUR 8.04 example does **not** include the biggest cost drivers:

- signature maintenance for shared mailboxes,
- manual updates of out-of-office replies,
- incorrect or outdated contact data,
- brand damage caused by CI/CD violations,
- regulatory and compliance risks,
- the marketing value of every outgoing email.

That is where the real return starts.
