Set-OutlookSignatures, the Benefactor Circle add-on, and the Outlook add-in are now available in **version 4.30.0**. This release brings massive capabilities to our Outlook add-in with new event-driven triggers, safeguards for your deployment process, and key stability fixes.

### Outlook Add-in: New Launch Events & Automation

We have significantly expanded the automation capabilities of the Outlook add-in by introducing support for several highly requested launch events. These events allow you to enforce and adapt signatures dynamically based on user actions:

- **`OnMessageSend` & `OnAppointmentSend`:** Enforces a specific signature right after the user clicks "Send", completely overriding manual changes. The signature is applied client-side, ensuring the sender can see the final result directly in their _Sent Items_ folder.
- **`OnMessageCompose` & `OnAppointmentOrganizer`:** Triggers when composing emails or managing appointments, including when editing drafts or existing calendar items.
- **`OnSensitivityLabelChanged`:** Enables dynamic signature assignment based on the item's sensitivity label via `CUSTOM_RULES_CODE` (requires a mailbox with at least an E5 license).

> 💡 **Note:** All new launch events are disabled by default and can be manually enabled in `run_before_deployment.ps1`. To keep Outlook running fast, our optimized `manifest.xml` generation now automatically excludes any unused launch events to prevent unnecessary UI messages.

### Smarter Deployment & Custom Rules

To make deployments safer and rule-bending more flexible, we've upgraded our tooling and scripting properties:

- **Manifest Guardrails:** `run_before_deployment.ps1` now displays configuration differences between your new local `manifest.xml` and the online version, and will actively warn you if your local version number is lower than what is currently live.
- **Expanded `customRulesProperties`:** When writing `CUSTOM_RULES_CODE`, you can now utilize `triggeredByLaunchEventName` to detect what started the script, alongside `sensitivityLabelGuid` and `sensitivityLabelName` to react to data classification changes.

### Core Enhancements & Fixes

- **PowerShell 5.1 Compatibility:** Corrected the assembly resolver code in `sample code\SimulateAndDeploy.ps1` to ensure it works reliably across all PowerShell 5.1 environments.
- **Documentation Update:** Updated the FAQ section on how to [Keep users from adding, editing and removing signatures](https://set-outlooksignatures.com/faq#keep-users-from-adding-editing-and-removing-signatures).
